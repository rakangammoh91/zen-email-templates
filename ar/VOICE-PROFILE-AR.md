## 8. What the data says Seham's voice IS

1. **Peer, not mother, not brand.** She writes "صاحبتك" (your friend) and "بنت وحدة" (one girl), never "حبيبتي / يالغالية". The register is friend-texting-friend, not shopkeeper-to-customer. The feminine kasra is a signal: this is woman-to-woman, not company-to-consumer.

2. **Short-breath prose.** 40% of sentences are ≤6 words; 84% ≤12 words. Fragments and em-dashes are the primary rhythm device ("وصلكِ؟ قوليلي." · "يوم شعركِ مو متعاون — سهام"). No long MSA clauses. No conjunctive walls.

3. **Founder-authority is the strongest open-rate lever.** "رسالة من مؤسس زِن هير" is the single highest-CTR AR pattern in the corpus (0.43-0.45% CTR vs 0.13-0.28% for product subjects). The brand's biggest asset is Seham's signature, not product copy.

4. **Direct asks, not soft nudges.** 85% of imperatives are 2nd-person feminine ("شوفي / قوليلي / كلّميني / خبّريني"). She tells the reader what to do next, but the verbs are conversational ("tell me", "talk to me") rather than transactional ("buy now", "order today").

5. **Objection-handling via dialect questions.** The highest-opening organic subjects are Khaleeji-dialect questions: "وش رايك لو…؟" / "ماذا لو ما طلبت…؟" These beat declarative product subjects on open rate by ~10 points.

## 10. EN note (voice drift signal)

EN top-10 open at 56-68% (vs AR 55-80%) but convert at 0.12-0.35% (vs AR 0.02-0.15%) and generate **revenue per recipient up to 7.72 SAR vs AR peak 1.87**. If Seham wants to scale revenue and not just reach, the EN voice deserves a parallel Voice Profile — it is a different audience, likely different register (more transactional, less Khaleeji), and the data suggests it is a more commercially efficient lane despite smaller list size. Do NOT blend the two voices in one template.
