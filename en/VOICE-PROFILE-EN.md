## 2. Dialect / register fingerprint

**Register verdict:** **Conversational American/British English with light Saudi cultural markers.** No Hijazi transliteration in EN. No MSA loanwords. Not luxury-brand register ("exquisite / artisanal / bespoke" = 0 occurrences). Not aggressive direct-response ("Buy now!" / "Act fast!" = 0 in top 10, though "Hurry!" and "ENDS TONIGHT!" appear in the broader corpus).

| Register marker | Count in top 10 | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Culturally neutral English | 10/10 | All subjects readable to a non-Arab English speaker |
| Explicit Saudi/Arab reference | 1/10 | "100,000+ arab women" |
| Ramadan / Eid lexicon | 3/10 | "Ramadan" appears 3× in top 10 subjects |
| Founder-authority trigger | 1/10 | "a message from Rakan" |
| All-caps shouting | 0/10 top 10; ~8/81 corpus | Top 10 does NOT use ALL-CAPS; broader corpus does (e.g., "ENDS TONIGHT!", "WHITE FRIDAY IS LIVE") — those are lower performers |
| Contractions | frequent | "didn't", "don't", "it's", "We're" — normal spoken contractions throughout |
| British vs American | mixed | "colour" not observed; "color" is used — American-leaning |

**What's different from AR:** the EN voice drops ALL dialect markers. No "يا بنت / صاحبتك / وش رايك" equivalents (no "Hey girl", no "sis", no "babe"). The register is closer to a **thoughtful friend / stylist recommendation** than the peer-girlfriend tone of AR. Dialect cues like explicit feminine pronouns are untranslatable in English and are not replaced by any equivalent gendered marker.

**Confidence:** Medium (subjects only; no body to confirm whether opener pattern and sign-offs carry the same register).

## 8. What the data says the EN voice IS

1. **Direct, not flowery. Not luxury. Not desert-kingdom exotic.** Zero top-10 subjects invoke "artisan / premium / exquisite / bespoke" luxury register. The voice treats EN subscribers as **smart women making a practical hair decision**, not as a luxury clientele. Evidence: "Busy day? These 3 hairstyles keep up" · "Still not sure? Here are the 5 questions we get most" · "Welcome Ramadan 🌙 Enjoy 15% Off + A Mystery Gift". Register = **conversational / practical / slightly-warm** (~peer stylist), not high-luxury.

2. **Emoji is allowed in EN and does not penalize.** 60% of top-10 use a soft single emoji at end-of-subject. This inverts the corpus-level finding (which is driven by AR). **EN voice spec: one soft emoji, at end of subject, optional.**

3. **The founder-authority lever exists in EN but it's Rakan OR "our founder" — not Seham — and the replication is inconclusive.** One EN "a message from Rakan 💛" sent Mar 6 got **1.00% CTR** (top CTR of the top 10, ~4× EN cohort baseline) but 0 conversions and 0 revenue on that send. The Nov 14 "A message from our founder" got 0.47% CTR and again 0 conversions. Compare AR: "رسالة من مؤسس زِن هير" is the single highest CTR AR pattern at 0.43–0.45% and produces real revenue. **The EN founder lever opens and clicks but has not yet produced a revenue conversion** in the 180d window — sample n=2 EN "founder" sends, both in free traffic conditions. Treat as **hypothesis, not proven pattern**.

4. **Question subjects win.** 3/10 top-10 EN are questions. Questions also show the highest observed CTR outside of the "message from" subjects ("Ready in 5 Minutes: 3 Everyday Looks" tangential; "5 things every first-timer worries about" 0.38%). EN question format replicates the AR finding.

5. **Short-breath prose hypothesis holds at subject level.** All top-10 EN subjects are ≤11 words. Median 6. 10/10 are one-thought sentences. **Cannot confirm for body** without HTML, but AR's short-breath rhythm appears carried into EN subject craft.

## 10. AR note (what AR can't tell us about EN)

Things that only the EN cohort reveals:

- **EN monetizes far better per recipient.** Top 10 EN RPR = 7.72 · 4.28 · 3.41 · 3.27 · 3.04. Peak AR RPR = 1.87. EN is ~2–7× more efficient on small engaged list. Confirms `patterns.md` cluster 1 finding at the voice-cohort level.
- **EN tolerates emoji.** AR doesn't. This is a voice-level divergence, not just a subject-style tweak. Do not migrate AR's no-emoji rule to EN.
- **EN sign-offs drop the "co-founder" title.** AR's canonical close is `سهام · شريك مؤسس زن هير`; EN's is `Seham from Zen Hair` (or `Rakan from Zen Hair`). The EN list doesn't require the credential stamp.
- **EN has a second founder voice ("Rakan").** AR does not.
- **EN seasonal lexicon tilts heavier on "Ramadan" + "gift" than on any hair-care topic.** 3 of top 10 EN winners are Ramadan-gift-framed — the EN list buys into the culturally-ambient Ramadan moment without needing Hijazi-voice framing.
